Blindfold

Keep the mare blindfolded as much as possible and use the tightest option that still allows them to do what is required of them. eg.

  • When stalled, opaque
  • When carting, unblinded
  • Whip circle, medium to thin
  • Traverse, unblinded
  • Flower, medium to thin
  • Hanging around, medium to thin

Chat

Keep the mare guessing and unable to understand what is being said whenever possible. Don't lift restrictions like mare hearing or use chat bypasses unless the information is critical for the mare.

  • Block Start IM
  • Use @stopim
  • Block Send IM

Reins

Use reins with the mare as much as possible, 5-10 meters is ideal.

  • Stall, 2 meters
  • Carting, 5 meters
  • Walking around, 5-10 meters
  • Intimate, 2-5 meters

Introduction

Let's start with defining what a mare is in the context of Second Life pony play and this guide. In this context, a mare is any heavily restricted pony, regardless of whether they are male or female. It is not about the physical appearance of the pony, but about the level of restriction and the level of control the trainer has over the pony.

It's important to remember that there's no one size fits all for mares, mare life can be very lonely and boring at times; this boredom can be leveraged by the trainer.

Finally, the three cards up top are a quick reference and suggestions for how to handle the mare. It's not meant to be used like a slave, but as a guide.

Chat

Chat control is a big part of controlling a mare, and can be used to tease and torment the mare.

Mare hearing is one tool that is very effective, many stables in Second Life have tools that offer this feature, if yours doesn't, you can get The Unhearing by Mara Lucente, this can be integrated with Anavision Rescript1, or used standalone. Mare hearing captures the local chat around the mare and replaces most words with gibberish, while passing a very limited set of words a mare can understand. For example; You say "pony or a mare?", what the mare hears is "pony ʵʵ ᶶ mare?". Here the words "or" and "a" are replaced with gibberish, while the words "pony" and "mare" are passed through to the mare.

With mare hearing, it's ideal if you have someone else with you to chat with. This way, you reinforce that the mare isn't able to understand human speech. This for some mares is very frustrating, while others, like myself, find it cathartic.

IM control is another effective tool, limit who and when they can chat with others and make them focus on the task at hand, not chit-chatting with others.

Start IM is the most basic form, this makes it so you can control whether or not they can start an IM session with others, and even optionally who they can and can't start the IMs with. When you have a mare with this restriction and you open a chat with them, you can close their side of the chat using the command @stopim.

Send IM lets you control if they can send IMs at all even if they have an IM window open, like with Start IM, you can also control who they can optionally send IMs to.

Receive IM lets you control if they can receive IMs at all, like with Start IM, you can also control who they can optionally receive IMs from.

Using Start IM is something I recommend for everybody as well as mare hearing. Whereas Send IM is something you should consider only for some mares, and Receive IM is for specific situations and usually with an exception, especially for you and the current keyholder, who they might need to receive messages from. You can for example with Anavision Rescript use the $KEY variable to set exceptions for the key holder for whenever they are holding the reins of the mare.

Blindfold

The blindfold is another very powerful tool, making sure the mare's focus is exactly where you want it to be.

Personally, I'm a fan of using the blindfold in a more realistic way, as much as Second Life allows, while still keeping it enjoyable for the mare.

For this I prefer to use Anavision, as it gives immense control over the mare's vision.

Below is a list of the various settings I prefer to use and when to use them.

  • When stalled, opaque
  • When carting, unblinded unless we're doing blind carting, then opaque
  • Whip circle, medium to thin
  • Traverse, unblinded
  • Flower, medium to thin
  • Hanging around, medium to thin
  • Intimate chilling, as tight as possible where they still can see the trainer

Reins

The use of reins is something I think is very important, it reinforces that their place is by the trainer's side, or in their stall, and that their enrichment is dependent on the trainer.

The only times a mare may be off the reins is for example when doing flower dressage or silent dressage, otherwise they should be on a lead that is appropriate. Personally I prefer a 5 meter long lead, as this forces the mare to be present at all times while walking, rather than being some plushie the trainer drags around half-AFK.2

Though having a pony heel and follow without a lead is nice for the trainer, it undermines the experience for the mare, and I'd discourage doing so.

Restrictions

A key part of a mare's life is the loss of all agency and control, that includes things like being able to choose what to wear, read notecards, work on scripts, etc. To deliver on this, leverage many if not most of the RLV restrictions available in whatever system you have available to you, but be cognizant that there can be cases where those restrictions have to be lifted, so the method should allow other trainers to undo the restrictions if needed. Here's a short list of restrictions I prefer to use:

  • No Inventory
  • No Edit
  • No Fly
  • No Touch Self
  • No Reach
  • No Far Cam
  • No Touch Others
  • No Touch Attach
  • No Start IM
  • No Notecard
  • No Edit Script
  • No Location
  • No World Map
  • No Mini Map
  • No Name Tags
  • No Show Hovertext World
  • No Show Nearby

Training

Training is a big part of a mare's life, as a trainer, don't feel like you need the mare's opinion, they are animals. If you wonder if they have time, just ask them if they are available for an activity and give them a rough time frame if you know that, and then they can answer yes or no.

If you have any long-term OOC discussion you need to have with the mare rather than disabling mare Hearing, just open an IM window and ask them there. If need be, lift any restrictions necessary so you can have that OOC discussion.

Most of the time, the mare does not actually need to listen to any chatter, and by disabling the mare hearing, you're just undermining the experience for the mare. They might as well just be a regular pony without those restrictions that mare hearing imposes. So try to keep it on as much as possible because that's the point of being a mare.

If you're unsure what activities to do, use a Wheel of Fortune, dice roll, or something similar.

Being a mare

As mentioned earlier, being a mare is deeply lonely, especially in the stall, so as a trainer, feel free to grab them and take them out of the stall, even if the goal is just to chill or watch someone doing something. For the mare, it's much more enjoyable than being left alone in the stall, it makes them feel less like they are forgotten.

Being a mare is all about being treated like an animal, an extreme loss of agency and control. Think of it almost as TPE-lite, so as the submissive, you have to make sure you are comfortable with that.

Remember that being a mare isn't a punishment, it's a kink. Personally, I think others should try it at least once, for a couple of days, so you can understand how it is and how to leverage as a trainer.
